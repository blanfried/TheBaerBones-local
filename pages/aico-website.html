Project Summary

AICO, a $100 million/year furniture manufacturer, had three user groups—sales representatives/retailers, internal teams, and consumers—all dealing with an outdated legacy ASP website.

Research included observational studies, top-down stakeholder interviews, and surveys across all personas. Users expecting luxury found an ugly website, sales reps lacked quick product information access, IT had sole control with self-proclaimed zero design sensibility, and marketing couldn't update campaigns independently.

The solution involved a threefold user flow accounting for all personas whilst achieving executive aesthetic requirements and user efficiency needs. It combined Webflow with a strategic backend integration involving a proprietary php application, allowing marketing to have control and satisfying IT's security protocols.

The Constraint Environment

Due to overlapping constraints in security, aesthetics, design access, outdated architecture, variety of users, and 25+ years years of backend workarounds with zero SOPs, vendors quoted over $200,000 for a complete redesign; I delivered it for under $1000.

The Journey

There were five distinct phases: initial stakeholder alignment and constraint mapping, comprehensive user research across multiple personas, technical architecture development bridging incompatible systems, integrated design implementation balancing competing requirements, and systematic deployment with measurable organizational impact.

Phase 1

Stakeholder Alignment & Constraint Mapping

Initial engagement with executive leadership established the scope of organizational transformation required beyond standard website redesign.

Comprehensive constraint mapping revealed the intersection of budget limitations, security requirements, and operational independence needs across departments.

Phase 2

Multi-Persona Research

Systematic investigation across four distinct user groups revealed fundamental misalignment between system architecture and actual workflow patterns.

Research methodology included observational studies, structured interviews, and behavioral analysis to identify root organizational inefficiencies.

Phase 3

Architecture Development

Development of proprietary bridging technology enabled seamless connection between modern design tools and legacy security infrastructure.

This technical innovation resolved the fundamental incompatibility between visual design requirements and operational constraints.

Phase 4

Unified Design Implementation

Implementation of context-sensitive interface systems accommodated diverse user needs without compromising luxury brand positioning or operational efficiency.

Design decisions integrated accessibility standards with executive aesthetic requirements through adaptive navigation architecture.

Phase 5

Deployment and Optimization

Systematic rollout included comprehensive training, performance monitoring, and iterative refinement based on user feedback.

Results demonstrated successful transformation of organizational workflows while maintaining all original constraint parameters.
