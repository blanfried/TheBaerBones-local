About Me

‍

I grew up in Newport Beach, California, with missionary parents whose work took us to many places. Along the way, I met people who seemed to carry a sense of purpose, even through hardship. This made me wonder: where does enduring purpose come from?

After high school, I moved to Paris for college, majoring in Philosophy and Psychology. My search for purpose guided me throughout these years. Later, teaching English in the French Basque Country, I found purpose through connection—with the environment, with others, and with myself. But while I found purpose in these experiences, I hadn’t yet discovered it in a professional sense or in a way that allowed me to give back through meaningful work.

This search took me to Birmingham, UK, where I pursued an MA in Ethics and Philosophy of Religion. I found purpose in crafting complex arguments and creating through philosophy. Yet, much of the work was done in isolation. Though I loved the creation, I missed the connection.

That’s when I came across a coding bootcamp at the University of Birmingham. Coding brought logic, creation, and connection with others together. Over six months, I worked with others to build apps and websites. I found purpose in collaboration, but it still felt fleeting—like something was missing.

After the bootcamp ended, I sold my car and traveled for six months to reflect. Purpose was by no means absent from my life. What I lacked was a professional purpose—a purpose I could grow with and use to give back through my work. That’s when TheBaerBønes came into focus: a personal brand that lets me build professional purpose, both for myself and others.

TheBaerBønes is both my own vehicle for professional purpose and it allows me to help others uncover the core of their professional purpose. Together, we can all create something meaningful and fulfilling.

My Qualifications

Qualifications

Lead Website Designer & Manager at Global Luxury Furniture Brand

I led the design and development of AICO’s (Michael Amini) website, a global multi-million-dollar furniture brand. I manage ongoing updates, redesigns, and optimizations to align with marketing campaigns and evolving business needs.

Tech Team Head at Vinissimoenology

I was the Tech Team Head for a beverage inventory SAAS start-up based in Italy, called "Vinissimo". I was the primary UI/UX Designer and Frontend Engineer. I managed the tech team as a whole.

Whilst I eventually left this role for a new opportunity with AICO, I contributed much to the future development of their tech.

Freelance UI/UX & Software Engineer

Before starting TheBaerBønes, I was contracted to create, design, and manage websites and software for Interior Design, Music Production, Musical Talent Acquisition, Chamber of Commerce, Agritourism, Demolition, Construction, Artisan Catering, European Cooking Course, and Vacation Rental companies.

Full-Stack Coding Bootcamp

I kicked off my website journey with a rigorous six month coding bootcamp through my graduate school, the University of Birmingham.

Alongside my course mates, I learned how to make websites, applications, and other software tidbits. It was incredibly fast paced and much of our cohort didn't make it through to the end.

After completing the course, I started making websites for my friends and family to smooth out my rough edges and develop my design sense.

MA Philosophy

I received an MA in Philosophy with a focus on Ethics and Philosophy of Religion from The University of Birmingham, which was the top Philosophy department in the UK whilst I was attending.*

Philosophy translates to creative thinking, effective copywriting, rapid problem solving, and a deep understanding of how people think. I can communicate skillfully with those I collaborate with, and I can write convincing copy.*

BA Psychology & Philosophy

I double majored in Psychology and Philosophy at The American University of Paris.

Psychology offers insight into the forces that shape human behavior and guide decision-making. I'd like to say this helps my creation process when I make a website that can connect with its audience in a meaningful manner.

1200 Hours 1-to-1 Teaching

With nearly 1,200 hours of experience teaching Philosophy, Writing, English, Psychology, Web Development (HTML, CSS, JS), French, and Spanish in a 1-to-1 format, I’ve developed the ability to connect quickly with new people and adapt to their needs.
