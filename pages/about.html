About Me

‍

My fascination with contradictions started with my undergraduate thesis where I reconciled scientific determinism with free-will, then in my graduate dissertation solving the logical paradox of three-in-one unity.

After my Philosophy MA and coding program at the University of Birmingham, I spent years teaching logic and philosophy one-on-one while freelance coding. I operated between deep human interaction and technical work.

I continued in this tension as Technical Lead for an Italian SaaS startup, Vinissimoenology. I created their initial inventory system, translated sommelier expertise into technical requirements, and co-architected their AWS infrastructure.

My current role as Solutions Architect at AICO involves bridging IT, Marketing, and Executive departments while modernizing their integrated website platform. I led the complete redesign of their client-facing interface and internal systems, managing thousands of SKUs while designing integration patterns between legacy ASP code and modern architecture. The new system maintains human touchpoints across departments while operating systematically at enterprise scale.

TheBaerBones represents the full application of this methodology. I remain at the front, maintaining human connection with every client while my automated system operates beneath the surface. Starting with each client's vision, I build their system from that foundation. You’re positioned at the front, with an automated scalable system supporting you underneath.

Philosophy Background

I majored in Philosophy at The American University of Paris and received an MA in Philosophy with a focus on Ethics from the top Philosophy department in the UK, The University of Birmingham.

A study of 600,000+ students found philosophy majors "consistently rank highest in verbal and logical reasoning" with significantly larger gains on critical thinking measures versus non-philosophy majors. They also rank among the highest on standardized and logic tests. Philosophy develops skills in research and analysis, writing, persuasive communication, and learning ability different subjects.

These abilities directly translate to solutions architecting, where research, logical reasoning, communication, and the capacity to synthesize complex problems are essential.
